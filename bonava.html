Bonava

Client:
Bonava is a residential development company in Northern Europe, which develops and sells homes and neighbourhoods to consumers and investors in selected markets

Case:
How is the neighbourhood page perceived in relation to user needs, and how can it be improved?

Problem

During usability testing it became clear that the transition from Swedenâ€™s leading property website Hemnet, to that of the object page on Bonava, was jarring. Users often dropped out or became confused as they could not find the information they were seeking. My team and I developed a redesign of the object page that would help users find key information promptly.

Solution

As property images, floor plans and home facts were the most sought-after details in our interviews and user tests, our design placed these at the top of the page, while information about the neighbourhood was given lower priority.

also made some design changes to the header to make the first impression more "minimal" so that users will not be overloaded with information at the critical moment they search for the essential information.

We reduced the area of some elements, such as the text describing the property which, in the new design, shares space with the home facts. We also made the PDF documents smaller and closer in proximity to the home facts.
